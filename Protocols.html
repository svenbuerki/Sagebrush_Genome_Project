Protocols

1 Chromosome counting

1.1 Objective

Counting chromosomes of Artemisia tridentata using root meristems of individual lines maintained in vitro at BSU. This approach will help ascertaining the ploidy level (2n) of our individual lines.

1.2 Plant material

Young whitish roots are sampled from individuals cultured in vitro either at Growth or Rooting phases.

1.3 Sampling and fixing tissue

  1. Sample young whitish roots and place them in a Petri dish.
  2. Use achromatic spindle inhibitors to stop mitotic divisions (at metaphase) and contract chromosomes. This can be done following two approaches:
    • Treat roots with saturated solution of water and α-monobromonaphtalene for 3 hours at room temperature. Start by homogenizing the stock solution and adding supernatant to cover roots in the Petri dish.
    • Treat roots in a mix of water and crushed ice (4°C) for 10 hours.
  3. Plant tissue fixation and conserving chromosomes.
    • Prepare a falcon tube with the solution of ethanol:acetic acid (3:1). Add few drops of the acetocarmin solution and one drop of iron acetate. Homogenize the solution by gently shaking the falcon tube.
    • Transfer roots into falcon tube and store at room temperature for 1-2 days.

1.4 Staining tissue

  1. Pour acetocarmin solution in a porcelain crucible and add roots. Cover crucible with a lid.
  2. Prepare a Petri dish filled with 45% acetic acid. This will be used to cool down stained roots and preserve them until chromosome numbers is established.
  3. Gently heat solution in porcelain crucible with a Bunsen burner for ca. 2 minutes. Avoid boiling! This will stain chromosomes in red.
  4. Pour content of porcelain crucible into Petri dish.

1.5 Squashing tissue

  1. Put a drop of the solution in the Petri dish on a microscope slide and add a root tip in it.
  2. Remove the root cap under the scope using tweezers.This will facilitate the squash and observation of chromosomes.
  3. Cover with a plastic coverslip and use handle of tweezers to squash the tissue. Go gently!
  4. Observe under microscope and take picture to validate chromosome count.

1.6 Preparing stock solutions

Recipes to prepare solutions presented here were adapted from this document (in french).

1.6.1 3:1 ethanol:acetic acid solution

  • Mix one volume of acetic acid for three volumes of ethanol. Store at 4°C.

1.6.2 Acetocarmin solution

For 1000 mL for acetocarmin solution:

  1. Measure 450 mL of 45% glacial acetic acid and pour in XXX (a vessel that we can heat). See Figure in this document.
  2. Measure 550 mL of distilled water and add to vessel.
  3. Weigh 5 gr. of carmin (Fisher C579-25). Carmine is a basic dye that is prepared from the insect Coccus cacti.
  4. Add boileezers, and reflux for 2-3 hours.
  5. Filter into dark bottles and store at 4°C.

This solution can be stored for a long time. Staining can be intensified by adding ferric chloride (FeCl2·6H2O); add 5 mL of a 10 % ferric chloride solution per 100 mL of % acetocarmine.
